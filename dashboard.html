📖 Signal Glossary
Setup Signals — Pre-Move Patterns
Trend Pullback
Trend Pullback
A stock in a confirmed uptrend has pulled back to a key support level (20-day or 50-day moving average) on declining volume. This is one of the cleanest setups in trading — you're buying a temporary dip in a stock that's already proven it wants to go higher.
Triggers when: Price is above the 50-day MA (uptrend confirmed) AND is within 5–7% of the 20/50-day MA AND RSI has cooled to the 25–55 range AND volume on the pullback is lower than average (healthy, not panic selling).
Watch for: A reversal candle at support (hammer, engulfing) with volume picking back up. That's your entry signal.
Volatility Breakout
Volatility Breakout
The stock has been trading in a very tight range (low volatility / price compression) near its recent highs. This kind of coiling action often precedes a sharp move. Think of it like a spring being compressed — the tighter the range, the bigger the potential move when it breaks.
Triggers when: Bollinger Band width is in the bottom 30% of its recent history (compression confirmed) AND price is within 8% of its 52-week high AND technical score is strong.
Watch for: A close above the recent resistance level on 2x or more the average volume. That's the breakout confirmation.
Short Squeeze
Short Squeeze
A large percentage of this stock's float is sold short — meaning many traders are betting the price goes down. If the stock starts moving up instead, those short sellers are forced to buy shares to cover their losses, which pushes the price even higher. This can cause explosive, rapid moves.
Triggers when: Short float is above 15% AND days-to-cover is above 3 AND price is turning upward AND volume is accelerating above the 20-day average.
Watch for: Sustained volume with the stock unable to be pushed lower by sellers. Short squeezes can move 20–50%+ in days but can also reverse violently. Size carefully.
Earnings Drift
Earnings Drift (PEAD)
Post-Earnings Announcement Drift — one of the most well-documented patterns in finance. When a company beats earnings estimates, the market often under-reacts initially and the stock continues drifting higher for days or weeks after the announcement as more investors pile in.
Triggers when: Quarterly earnings growth is above 15% AND the stock is not yet overbought (RSI below 65) AND both technical AND fundamental scores are strong (the beat hasn't been fully priced in yet).
Watch for: Continued institutional buying (increasing volume on up days). The drift typically plays out over 2–4 weeks.
Score Definitions (0–1 scale)
Upside Score
The master score — a weighted combination of all six factor groups below. Think of it as an overall "how interesting is this opportunity" rating. Above 0.65 is high. The bot only alerts when this clears the configured threshold AND all other gates pass.
Risk Score
How dangerous this position is to hold. Combines liquidity (how easy it is to get in/out without moving the price), daily volatility (ATR as % of price), and gap history (how often it makes large overnight moves). Lower is safer. The bot filters out anything above 0.60.
Tech Score
Pure price action. Looks at trend alignment (price vs 20/50/200-day MAs), volatility compression, RSI, and whether volume is accumulating on up days vs down days. High = stock is in a technically healthy position.
Fund Score
Business quality. Revenue and earnings growth rates, gross margin trends, valuation relative to sector peers (PEG ratio, EV/Sales), and balance sheet health (debt levels, cash runway). High = growing business at a reasonable valuation.
Sent Score
Market perception. Aggregates news headline sentiment (positive/negative keyword scoring), analyst upgrade/downgrade activity and price target changes, and options flow signals (call-to-put ratios, unusual options volume). High = the market is warming up to this name.
Struct Score
Supply/demand dynamics. Looks at short interest (% of float sold short + days to cover), float size (smaller float = more explosive moves), institutional ownership, and insider buying activity. High = structural setup favors buyers over sellers.
Market Regime Labels
■ Risk On Strong — SPY in uptrend, broad market breadth healthy, VIX low. Best environment for momentum trades.
■ Risk On — Generally positive but not as clean. Most setups still work.
■ Choppy — Mixed signals. Stick to the highest-conviction setups only. Reduce position sizes.
■ Risk Off — Market under pressure. Defensive posture. The bot raises the bar for alerts.
■ Panic — Broad selloff. Very few signals will pass. Cash is a position.
